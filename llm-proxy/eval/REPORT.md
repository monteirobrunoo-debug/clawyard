# PII Redactor — Audit Report

Generated by `python -m eval.run_audit`. Re-run after every change to `redactor.py` or the fixture set. Numbers below are reproducible and committed so regressions show up in git diff.

## Executive summary

On the current fixture set (7 documents, 45 annotated spans across PT contracts, SAP exports, patent drafts and clean baselines):

- **90.5% recall** on categories the redactor is engineered to cover (19/21 sensitive spans scrubbed).
- **60.6% recall** when we honestly include categories the redactor does **not** yet cover — names, postal addresses, dates of birth, passports, patent/docket numbers.
- **0.0% false-positive rate** (0/12) — non-PII fields like SAP material numbers and cost centres pass through untouched.

> The gap between the two recall numbers is the honest thing to say in a sales conversation: PII classes the redactor claims to handle, it handles; classes it does not yet handle (names, addresses, DOB, IDs that are not NIF/IBAN/CC) are the next sprint, not a fait accompli. Regulated customers do not trust products that hide the second number.

## Remediation roadmap

Ordered by risk reduction per unit of engineering effort. Closing the first two items gets recall-on-all-spans above 90% for PartYard-style content.

1. **Named-entity pass for people & organisations** — use a lightweight NER model (spaCy `pt_core_news_md` runs inside the proxy in <50 ms for typical payloads). Redact `PER` and `ORG` spans to `[NAME_REDACTED]` / `[ORG_REDACTED]`. This alone covers the 4 leaked name spans plus any unseen variants.
2. **Portuguese postal-address pattern** — deterministic regex keyed on the `NNNN-NNN` postal-code format, expanded left to the street prefix (`Rua`, `Avenida`, `Travessa`, `Largo`, `Praceta`, `Rotunda`). Covers the 3 leaked address spans and generalises to other PT corpora.
3. **Domestic PT mobile/landline pattern** — add `\b[29]\d{8}\b` AFTER the NIF pass so mod-11 keeps priority. Catches the 2 supported-category phone FNs without re-introducing collisions.
4. **Passport / ID-with-letters pattern** — `[A-Z]{1,2}\d{6,8}` bounded by whitespace, applied after CC. Covers passport and patent-office docket numbers (`EP24-112233`, `CA123456`).
5. **URL-embedded basic-auth credentials** — `https?://[^\s:/]+:[^\s@/]+@` → scrub the `user:pass@` portion. Fires on the 1 annotated case and anywhere a reviewer pastes an auth-bearing URL into a ticket.
6. **DOB in ISO format** — `\b(19|20)\d{2}-[01]\d-[0-3]\d\b` is a cheap catch. Human-written dates (`12 de Julho de 1984`) fall out of the NER pass above.

Each of these is <1 day of work including the audit harness counterparts. Schedule against the regulated-customer pipeline, not in advance of it.
